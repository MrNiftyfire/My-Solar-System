The Solar System

The Sun

The sun is a star, specifically a yellow dwarf star,
that is the central object of our solar system.
It's a massive, hot ball of gas,
primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, and is the source of all the light and heat on Earth.
The sun's gravity holds the entire solar system together,
keeping planets and other objects in orbit.

Mercury

Mercury refers to both a chemical element and the planet closest to the Sun.
As a chemical element, it's a silvery-white metal that's liquid at room temperature,
known as quicksilver. As a planet, it's the smallest in the solar system,
known for its extreme temperatures and thin exosphere

Venus


Venus, the second planet from the sun, is often called Earth's "twin" due to its similar size and mass,
but it's a vastly different world.
Venus is the hottest planet in our solar system,
with a dense, toxic atmosphere and surface temperatures
hot enough to melt lead.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun,
the fifth largest planet in our solar system,
and the only one known to harbor life.
It is a terrestrial, rocky planet with an atmosphere primarily composed of nitrogen and oxygen,
and a surface that is about 71% covered in water.

Mars

Mars, also known as the "Red Planet,"
is the fourth planet from the Sun in our solar system.
It's a dusty, cold desert world with a very thin atmosphere.
Mars has seasons, polar ice caps, extinct volcanoes, and canyons.
It's about half the size of Earth
and has two moons, Phobos and Deimos.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system,
and the fifth from the Sun. It's a gas giant,
primarily composed of hydrogen and helium,
and has a mass more than 2.5 times that of all other planets combined.
Jupiter also has a prominent storm,
the Great Red Spot, which is larger than Earth.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun
and the second-largest in our solar system.
It's a gas giant,
primarily composed of hydrogen and helium,
and is famous for its stunning ring system.
Saturn is 9.5 times farther from the Sun than Earth
and takes about 29.5 Earth years to orbit the Sun.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is an ice giant,
known for its unique axial tilt,
which causes it to appear to spin on its side.
It has a blue-green color
due to methane in its atmosphere
and is surrounded by faint rings and at least 27 moons.

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun
in our solar system, according to NASA.
It's also known as an ice giant and is the densest of the giant planets, says Britannica.
Neptune is a striking blue color
due to the presence of methane in its atmosphere.

Kuiper Belt

The Kuiper Belt is a region beyond Neptune's orbit
in our solar system, a doughnut-shaped area filled with icy bodies and dwarf planets.
It's home to Pluto and many other large objects The Kuiper Belt
is considered a source of comets and is a remnant of the early solar system's formation.

Ceres

Ceres refers to multiple things:
a dwarf planet in the asteroid belt,
a goddess in Roman mythology,
and a community environment park in Melbourne, Australia.
In astronomy, Ceres is the largest object in the asteroid belt
and was the first to be discovered.
In Roman mythology, Ceres is the goddess of agriculture,
grain crops, and fertility.
Finally, CERES is a park and social enterprise in Melbourne
focused on environmental education and sustainability.

Pluto

Pluto is a dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt,
a region of icy bodies beyond Neptune.
Initially classified as a planet,
it was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006
by the International Astronomical Union (IAU)

Haumea

Haumea is a dwarf planet located in the Kuiper Belt,
a region beyond Neptune>. It's known for its unusual, elongated shape
and rapid rotation, making it one of the fastest-spinning objects
in our solar system. Haumea was discovered in 2003.

Makemake

Makemake is a dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt,
a region beyond Neptune
containing icy bodies left over from the solar system's formation.
It's a plutoid, meaning it's a dwarf planet
further from the Sun than Neptune,
and it orbits the Sun in about 305 Earth years.
Makemake is named after the creator god in Rapa Nui
mythology from Easter Island.

Eris

Eris is both a Greek goddess and a dwarf planet.
As a goddess, Eris is the personification of strife
and discord in Greek mythology.
She is known for being the sister of Ares
and being the source of conflict, including the Trojan War.
As a dwarf planet, Eris is a trans-Neptunian
object discovered in 2005, slightly smaller than Pluto,
and has at least one moon called Dysnomia.

Oort Cloud

The Oort Cloud is a hypothetical, vast spherical shell of icy bodies
thought to surround the solar system, located far beyond the Kuiper Belt.
It's believed to be the source of long-period comets,
which are not gravitationally bound to the solar system
and can travel from the Oort Cloud into the inner solar system.
